Every technology jump promises free time. It never delivers. The washing machine didn't give women leisure — it raised the bar for what *clean* meant. Email didn't reduce meetings. Smartphones didn't free anyone from desks. Americans don't spend saved time leisurely; they find new ways to consume. The only opt-out is to cease consuming, which isn't frugality — it's a philosophical position. The $100 bill passes back and forth and we call it GDP. The footnotes trace specific technologies and what actually happened to the free time they promised.
